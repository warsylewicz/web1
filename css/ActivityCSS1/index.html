Alberta, Canada

Welcome to the province of Alberta!

Location

Alberta is a province located in western Canada. It borders the provinces of British Columbia to the west and Saskatchewan to the east, as well as the Northwest Territories to the north and the US state of Montana to the south.

Nature

Alberta is known for its diverse and stunning natural beauty. From the Rocky Mountains to the prairies, the province is home to many national and provincial parks, lakes, rivers, and wildlife.

Cities

Alberta's largest cities include Calgary, Edmonton, and Red Deer. Each city has its own unique attractions, culture, and history.

Calgary

Calgary is the largest city in the province of Alberta and the fifth-largest city in Canada. It is located in the southern part of the province, about 80 km east of the Canadian Rockies. The city is known for its vibrant culture, friendly people, and thriving economy. It is home to many popular attractions, such as the Calgary Stampede, the Calgary Tower, and the Glenbow Museum. Calgary is also known for its proximity to Banff National Park, one of Canada's most popular tourist destinations.

Edmonton

Edmonton is the capital city of Alberta and the second-largest city in the province. It is located in central Alberta, about 300 km north of Calgary. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, lively arts and entertainment scene, and thriving economy. It is home to many popular attractions, such as the West Edmonton Mall, the Royal Alberta Museum, and the Muttart Conservatory. Edmonton is also known for its vibrant festivals, including the Edmonton International Fringe Festival and the Edmonton Folk Music Festival.

Red Deer

Red Deer is a city located in central Alberta, about halfway between Calgary and Edmonton. It is the third-largest city in the province, with a population of around 100,000 people. The city is known for its friendly community, natural beauty, and cultural attractions. Red Deer is home to many popular attractions, such as the Alberta Sports Hall of Fame and Museum, the Red Deer Museum and Art Gallery, and the Cronquist House. It is also known for its outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking, skiing, and golfing.

Quick Facts

  • Capital city: Edmonton
  • Population: over 4 million
  • Main industries: oil and gas, agriculture, tourism
  • Official flower: wild rose
  • Official bird: great horned owl
